# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Administrators, teachers, and students use this system in a lab/course-management context to manage semesters, experiment suites, schedules, grouping, templates, grades, and supporting files.

## Product Purpose

The product helps a physics lab/course team complete daily operational work quickly and reliably: importing students, assigning groups and teachers, maintaining schedules, entering grades, and downloading or uploading experiment materials. Success means common workflows feel direct, responsive, and predictable even on data-heavy pages.

## Brand Personality

Practical, calm, and trustworthy. The interface should feel like a dependable teaching-management tool, not a promotional site.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative dashboards, gratuitous animations, over-branded cards, and page-load choreography. Avoid interactions that surprise users or trigger extra reloads after a deliberate action.

## Design Principles

- Keep users in flow: avoid unnecessary page reloads, duplicate network calls, and full-screen blocking states.
- Prefer stable data views: preserve existing content while refreshing instead of blanking tables repeatedly.
- Make dense workflows readable: tables, filters, and actions should stay consistent across screens.
- Optimize the obvious bottlenecks first: repeated requests, oversized renders, and expensive generated HTML.
- Treat loading, empty, and error states as part of the workflow rather than interruptions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use familiar product UI patterns, keyboard-accessible controls, readable contrast, and reduced-motion-friendly transitions. Performance work should not remove labels, focus states, or feedback.
